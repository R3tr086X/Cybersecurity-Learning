# Workplace Security Policies

---

## Analyzed Workstations

### Executive Position

Executives have high privileges and access to critical information, which implies considerable risk. Their mobility and remote access require robust security policies to protect sensitive assets.

**Assets used:**
- Corporate laptop, mobile, and tablet  
- Elevated privilege credentials  
- Remote access to email, ERP, and sensitive documents

---

### Online Store Position

This position deals directly with external customers. Exposure to the Internet and handling personal data are essential, which involves significant security risks.

**Assets used:**
- Customer service PC  
- Customer and order data  
- Browser, email, and partial ERP access

---

### Administrative Position

The administrative position has access to sensitive data and central systems, implying high risks. Implementing security policies is crucial to protect personal information and billing.

**Assets used:**
- Desktop PC  
- Shared printer  
- Printed documents with sensitive data  
- Office software

---

## A.A.V.R. Analysis

### Executives

This analysis highlights vulnerabilities and threats faced by executives, including access to sensitive information and risks associated with losing mobile devices in public environments.

- **Assets:** mobile devices, credentials, strategic documents  
- **Risks:** information leakage, financial fraud, reputational damage  
- **Real scenario:** lost mobile at airport → full access to email and documents

### Online Store

The online store faces significant threats such as malware and social engineering attacks, which may lead to customer data leaks and loss of brand trust.

- **Main threats:** malware in attachments, fake websites, social engineering  
- **Risks:** customer data leaks, loss of trust, ERP access  
- **Real scenario:** customer sends infected file as payment proof

### Administrative

The administrative position presents significant risks due to access to sensitive data and potential unauthorized access, which could result in legal violations and security vulnerabilities.

- **Threats:** unauthorized physical access, infected USBs, ransomware  
- **Risks:** GDPR non-compliance, loss of invoice integrity  
- **Real scenario:** unlocked PC visible to public with customer data

## Security Principles

### Strong Passwords

Use complex passwords including uppercase, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols to ensure they are hard to guess and protect access to information.

### Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

Implementing MFA adds an extra layer of security, requiring users to verify their identity through multiple methods before accessing accounts.

### Safe Email Use

Responsible email use is crucial; do not open emails from unknown senders or click on suspicious links that may compromise security.

---

## General Policies

### Training

Phishing and security training is essential to educate employees on identifying malicious emails and links, reducing the risk of successful cyberattacks.

### Confidentiality

Control access to sensitive information, ensuring that only authorized personnel can view or manipulate critical data.

### Integrity

Protect data using security measures such as antivirus and regular updates, preventing malware and ensuring information remains intact.

### Availability

- **Backups:** ensure critical data recovery in case of attacks  
- **Software updates:** protect systems against vulnerabilities  
- **Continuous training:** prepare employees to detect and avoid threats

---

## Role-Specific Policies

### Executives

- Full device encryption  
- Mandatory VPN for remote access  

### Online Store

- Safe browsing and HTTPS use  
- Blocking unauthorized downloads  
- Network segmentation to isolate critical systems  

### Administrative

- Maintain clean desk and locked screens  
- Prohibit unauthorized USBs  
- Protect sensitive information in physical and digital documents

---

## Verification Checklist

### Executives

- Is the device encrypted?  
- Is MFA enabled for all access?  
- Is the mobile enrolled in MDM?  
- Is VPN used outside the office?  
- Has phishing training been received?  
- Is compliance checked every 6 months?

### Administrative

- Does the PC lock automatically?  
- Are unauthorized USBs disabled?  
- Are printed documents stored or destroyed?  
- Is the screen not visible to the public?  
- Is the employee trained in phishing?  
- Are accesses limited to sales/billing?

### Online Store

- Is MFA used for store access?  
- Is antivirus active and updated?  
- Is the browser updated?  
- Is the workstation on an isolated subnet?  
- Does the employee follow the procedure of not opening external attachments?  
- Is the screen not visible to the public?

## Frequently Asked Questions

- **Why can't I use personal devices like USBs or external drives?**  
  They may contain malware and can directly infect corporate systems.

- **What if I make an unintentional mistake, like opening a suspicious email?**  
  Reporting it immediately can prevent escalation of the incident.

- **Do these policies apply under pressure or tight deadlines?**  
  Yes. Attackers exploit stress, so following policies at all times is key.

---

## Consequences of Non-Compliance

- **Internal sanctions:** from warnings to loss of system access.  
- **Loss of trust:** damages relationships and the organization’s reputation.  
- **Global risks:** increases exposure to cyberattacks, endangering company data.

---

## Summary of Best Practices

- Always lock the device when away  
- Use strong passwords and MFA  
- Carefully review emails; avoid suspicious attachments  
- Keep the desk clean and protect sensitive information  
- Use only authorized devices and software  
- Report any incident immediately
